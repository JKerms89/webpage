PhD Research Project

In my PhD research project, I examine to what extent EU politics is an enabling condition for discursive nationalism. My analysis examines patterns of discourse before and during the COVID-19 pandemic in 8 newspapers from 4 EU member states. My research elucidates a somewhat paradoxical finding. On the one hand, discursive nationalism was more prevalent during the pandemic. On the other hand, discursive appeals to European solidarity and a European coalition of interests were more discernible. This supports Terror-Management-Theory (TMT) which posits that in moments of turmoil, people have a predilection for seeking ideological and worldview validation. This may partially explain why, for instance, right-wing political actors were more inclined to frame the crisis in national terms, and left-wing counterparts were instead more disposed to frame the crisis as a global emergency requiring international cooperation.
